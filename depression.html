1/8 Yoga poses for depression

❯

Tree Pose

Tree Pose (“Vrksasana”) may naturally cool off your body and could balance your fight, flight, or freeze response in times of anxiety and stress.
  1. From Mountain Pose, bring your hands to your hips and shift weight onto your right foot.
  2. Step your sole of your left foot to the inside of your right leg, below or above your knee.
  3. Find a focal point to affix your gaze to maintain balance.
  4. Breathe deeply for 5 to 7 breaths.
  5. Optional: Bring your hands together in front of your chest or raise your arms overhead.

Reclining Bound Angle Pose

The Reclining Bound Angle Pose (“Supta Baddha Konasana”) may help with headaches and even insomnia, in addition to calming tension and stress.
  1. Lie on your back with your knees bent, feet on the floor, and arms by your sides.
  2. Scoot your feet toward each other, moving heels and toes together, then press the soles of your feet together and let your knees fall out wide.
  3. Your arms can stay by your sides, or you can place them wider.
  4. Optional: Place one hand over your abdomen and the other hand over your chest.

3/8 Yoga poses for depression

❯

Mountain Pose

Mountain Pose (“Tadasana”) with upward salute can be an excellent yoga pose for improving posture and grounding yourself.
  1. Stand tall with a neutral spine and plant your feet about hips-distance apart, relaxing your arms by your sides with your palms facing forward.
  2. On an inhale, raise your arms overhead and press your palms together.
  3. On an exhale, lower your arms by your sides.
  4. Repeat for 3 to 5 cycles of breath.

4/8 Yoga poses for depression

❯

Low Lunge

The Low Lunge (“Anjaneyasana”) can help release tension in your hips and may improve mental focus.
  1. From Downward Dog, step your right foot forward just behind your right hand.
  2. Lower your back knee and untuck your toes.
  3. Draw your stomach in and engage your glutes as you lift your torso and arms overhead.
  4. Stay tall and upright in your spine, and gaze forward.
  5. Hold for 3 to 5 breaths, then release your hands back down.
  6. Step back to Downward Dog and repeat on the left side.
Illustrations by Alyssa Kiefer

❯

Knees to Chest Pose

Knees to Chest Pose (“Apanasana”) may reduce lower back pain and could be calming when experiencing symptoms of anxiety and depression.
  1. Lie on your back with your knees bent, feet on the floor, and arms by your sides.
  2. Draw your right knee to your chest and extend your left leg straight.
  3. Take a deep breath in, then exhale to draw your right knee in closer.
  4. Stay in this pose for a few breaths, and then switch sides.
Illustrations by Alyssa Kiefer












6/8 Yoga poses for depression

❯

Humble Warrior

The Humble Warrior (“Baddha Virabhadrasana”) asana is a popular pose for increasing balance and guiding focus inward to the present moment.
  1. From Downward Dog, step your right foot forward just behind your right hand.
  2. Pivot your back foot and anchor your back heel to the ground.
  3. Lengthen your torso and extend your arms behind you to interlace your palms.
  4. Take a deep breath in, then exhale to send your interlaced palms overhead. Your right shoulder should be on the inside of your right thigh.
  5. Hold for 3 to 5 breaths, release your palms, and step back to Downward Dog.
  6. Repeat on the other side.
Illustrations by Alyssa Kiefer












7/8 Yoga poses for depression

❯

Standing Forward Bend

The Standing Forward Bend (“Uttanasana”) can promote flexibility and relaxation, which can help you find release during depression and anxiety episodes.
  1. From Mountain Pose, hinge at your hips and fold forward until your fingertips touch the floor (or yoga blocks).
  2. Soften your knees and allow them to bend.
  3. Relax your neck and allow your head to feel heavy, gently turning your chin from side to side.
  4. Breathe deeply for 3 to 5 cycles of breath.
Illustrations by Alyssa Kiefer












8/8 Yoga poses for depression

Downward-Facing Dog

The classic yoga pose Downward-Facing Dog (“Adho Mukha Svanasana”) is a favorite full-body stretch asana, which may promote calmness.
  1. From a forward bend, plant your palms about shoulder-distance apart.
  2. Step your feet back one at a time as you reach your buttocks up and back. Your body should form an upside-down V-shape.
  3. Bend your knees to allow your spine to extend.
  4. Keep your neck relaxed as you gaze between your palms.
  5. Breathe deeply for 3 to 5 cycles of breath.
