How Meditation Helps with Depression

Meditation has been shown to be an effective tool in managing symptoms of depression. By focusing on the breath and letting go of distracting thoughts, meditation can help reduce negative thinking patterns and increase feelings of well-being. Additionally, meditation can help prepare the brain for stressful situations. For example, meditating for a few moments before a doctor's appointment or social situation can help shift the brain and body out of the stress response and into a state of relative calm.

There are many different types of meditation that can be helpful for depression, including:

  • Mindfulness meditation: focuses on being present in the moment and observing thoughts and feelings without judgment
  • Loving-kindness meditation: involves generating feelings of love and kindness toward oneself and others
  • Transcendental meditation: uses a mantra or sound to help quiet the mind and achieve a deep state of relaxation
  • Yoga: combines physical postures with breathing techniques and meditation
