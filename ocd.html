Symptoms of Obsessive Compulsive Disorder 
    Red flags that may suggest an eating disorder include:
    a)Skipping meals or snacks or making excuses for not eating.
    b)Having a very limited diet that hasn't been prescribed by a trained medical professional.
    c)Too much focus on food or healthy eating, especially if it means not participating in usual events, such as sports banquets, eating birthday cake or dining out.
    d)Making own meals rather than eating what the family eats.
    e)Withdrawing from usual social activities.
    f)Frequent and ongoing worry or complaints about being unhealthy or overweight and talk of losing weight.
    g)Frequent checking in the mirror for what are thought to be flaws.
    h)Repeatedly eating large amounts of foods.
    i)Using dietary supplements, laxatives or herbal products for weight loss.
    j)Exercising much more than the average person. This includes not taking rest days or days off for injury or illness or refusing to attend social events or other life events because of wanting to exercise.
    k)Calluses on the knuckles from reaching fingers into the mouth to cause vomiting.
    l)Problems with loss of tooth enamel that may be a sign of repeated vomiting.
    m)Leaving during meals or right after a meal to use the toilet.
    n)Talk of depression, disgust, shame or guilt about eating habits.
    o)Eating in secret.
