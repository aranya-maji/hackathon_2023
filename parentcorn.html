Parents' Corner

What are the signs of depression in children?

Signs of depression in children can include persistent sadness or irritability, feelings of hopelessness or worthlessness, decreased interest in activities they used to enjoy, changes in appetite or sleeping patterns, difficulty concentrating, and thoughts of self-harm or suicide.

How can I tell if my child is experiencing depression or just going through a normal phase?

While it is normal for children to experience mood swings and changes in behavior as they grow and develop, it is important to pay attention to the duration and intensity of their symptoms. If your child has been persistently sad, irritable, or withdrawn for more than two weeks and it is affecting their daily life, it may be a sign of depression.

What should I do if I think my child is experiencing depression?

If you suspect your child is experiencing depression, it is important to seek professional help. Talk to your child's pediatrician or a mental health professional who specializes in working with children and adolescents. They can help diagnose the condition and develop a treatment plan.

Is depression in children treatable?

Yes, depression in children is treatable with a combination of therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes. Early intervention is key to successful treatment outcomes.

What lifestyle changes can help alleviate symptoms of depression in children?

Encouraging regular exercise, a healthy diet, and a consistent sleep routine can help alleviate symptoms of depression in children. Additionally, creating a supportive and nurturing home environment, and engaging in enjoyable activities as a family can also be helpful.

Can changes in eating patterns be a sign of depression in children?

Yes, changes in eating patterns can be a sign of depression in children. Some children may experience a decrease in appetite, while others may overeat as a way to cope with their feelings.

Can depression in children lead to anger or frustration?

Yes, depression in children can manifest as irritability, anger, or frustration. Children may lash out or have temper tantrums in response to their feelings of sadness or hopelessness.
